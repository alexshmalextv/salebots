How to Sell a Helicopter

A practical, no-bullshit playbook on selling anything that “hurts to say out loud”: helicopters, real estate, yachts, industrial equipment, investment stones, consulting with six zeroes — without sleaze, pressure, or guru clichés.

Who this book is for

You sell (or want to sell) tickets with an extra zero: aircraft, real estate, equipment, luxury goods, investment products, complex services. You want a structured way to make a helicopter or a $1M contract feel like a logical step, not a gamble.

What you will learn

What really happens in the buyer’s head when an extra zero appears in the price; how to position yourself as a coordinator of a complex decision, not “a person with a price list”; how to work with risk, power and politics in big deals without manipulation or cheap tricks.

What you get

Mental models, structure of the deal and the funnel, roles at the table, frames and positioning, questions and scripts, plus a training block that turns the book from theory into a system you can run deals through.

What’s inside the playbook

The book is structured as a manual: you can read it front to back or jump directly into the part you need for your current deals — from mindset and people at the table to negotiations and drill.

Part One — The Head What happens when an extra zero appears; how to think about big deals.
Part Two — People on Both Sides of the Table Buyer’s psychology and the seller’s psychology: neither hero nor servant.
Part Three — The Process A deal as a route, not luck: where money comes from, how a conversation is built, where “almost” becomes “yes”.
Part Four — Regimen Training without which the book remains just text.
Part Five — The Person Who Runs the Game Frames, positioning, and a sales structure that stops confusing luck with a system.
Part Six — Selling to Busy People Lead generation and where money actually remains in your funnel.
Part Seven — Readiness to Pull the Trigger Preparation, research, dossier, ICP, meeting structure, diagnostics.
Parts Eight–Ten — Fine-tuning, negotiations, and drills Advanced salesmanship, objections, closing, and practical exercises (regimen) to install the system.

Ready to stop treating big deals as “luck”?

Use the playbook to rebuild just one active deal — the structure, questions and frames from the book are usually enough to pay for it with a single closed contract.
