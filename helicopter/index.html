How to Sell a Helicopter

A practical playbook on selling anything that “hurts to say out loud”: helicopters, aircraft, real estate, investment assets, heavy equipment, consulting with six zeroes — without pressure, sleaze, or guru clichés.

What it is

A system for high-ticket deals. Mental models, structures, and questions for negotiations where a single digit changes the conversation.

Where it applies

Helicopters and aircraft, real estate and development, yachts, industrial equipment, investment stones, complex services.

What you get

Clear roles at the table, conversation structures, and a training block to rebuild your current deals into a repeatable system.

Who the playbook is for

Not for beginners in sales. For people who already close deals — and want a clean system for numbers with an extra zero.

For whom

  • You sell (or want to sell) aircraft, real estate, equipment, luxury goods, investment products, or complex services.
  • You already know how to talk to clients, but hit a ceiling on big deals and can’t scale beyond intuition.
  • You coordinate several people on the buyer’s side and need a clear map of the decision process instead of “objection handling” tricks.

What you’ll learn

  • What really happens in the buyer’s head when an extra zero appears in the price.
  • How to position yourself not as “a person with a price list” but as a coordinator of a complex decision.
  • How to work with power, risk, and politics in big deals without manipulation or cheap tricks.
  • How to structure the deal, the timeline, and communication so that a large purchase becomes the logical, low-risk option.

What you get inside

  • Mental models for high-ticket negotiations and a clear structure of roles at the table.
  • Frameworks for meetings, calls, and email threads that move a serious buyer from “maybe” to decisions.
  • Frames and questions that surface real risks and constraints instead of chasing abstract “pain points”.
  • A practical training block to rebuild current deals and then your whole pipeline into a system.

What’s inside the playbook

The book is structured as a manual: you can read it front to back or go straight to the block you need for your current deal — from mindset and people at the table to structure, negotiations, and drills.

Part One — The Head
What happens the moment an extra zero appears; how to think about big deals and your role in them.
Part Two — People on Both Sides
Buyer’s psychology and the seller’s psychology: neither hero nor servant — how real decisions are made.
Part Three — The Process
A deal as a route, not luck: where people with money actually come from, how a serious conversation is built, how “almost” becomes “yes”.
Parts Four–Six — Regimen & Flow
Training that makes the material work, frames and positioning, a sales structure and funnel that stop confusing luck with a system, and lead generation that brings busy people to the table.
Parts Seven–Nine — Execution
Preparation, research, dossier, ICP; the structure of conversations and meetings; advanced salesmanship, weakness-based work instead of “pain points”; objections, negotiations, closing, handover, and repeat business.
Part Ten — Practical Training
Twenty-nine drills that walk through mindset, pipeline, structure, preparation, meetings, objections, and negotiations — so the system stays with you, not on the shelf.
This is not a “daily motivation” book. It is written for people who already know how to sell and want to move from sporadic wins to a repeatable system for big deals. You won’t find “101 closing techniques”; you will find a way to think, structure, and run conversations so that buying from you becomes the most rational move in the room.

About the author

The playbook is written by a practitioner who spent years in B2B sales and complex deals: from compressors and generators to real estate and investment assets. The material is based on real negotiations, failed and closed deals, not on abstract “sales theory”.

The goal of the book is simple: make you dangerous in big deals — in a good way. So that you clearly see what is actually happening at the table, where the money is lost, and what exactly to do next.

Take it to your next big-number deal

Use the playbook as a checklist before key meetings: to map the people at the table, clarify risks, and structure the route from the first serious conversation to handover and repeat business.
