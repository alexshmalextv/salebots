How to Sell a Helicopter

A practical playbook on selling anything that hurts to say out loud: helicopters, real estate, yachts, industrial equipment, investment assets, consulting with six zeroes — without sleaze, pressure, or guru clichés.

Who this book is for

  • You sell (or want to sell) tickets with an extra zero: aircraft, real estate, heavy equipment, luxury goods, investment products, complex services.
  • You already close deals, but feel you hit a ceiling on deal size or rely too much on intuition.
  • You coordinate several stakeholders on the buyer’s side and need a clear map of the decision process, not just “objection handling”.

What you will learn

  • What really happens in the buyer’s head when an extra zero appears in the price.
  • How to position yourself not as “a person with a price list” but as a coordinator of a complex decision.
  • How to work with risk, status, and conflict in big deals without manipulation or cheap tricks.

What you get inside

  • A complete structure of a high-ticket deal: from lead generation and first touch to handover and repeat business.
  • Frameworks, questions, and conversation structures for meetings, diagnostics, negotiations, and closing.
  • A practical drill section that lets you rebuild current deals and install the system into your own pipeline.

What’s inside the playbook

The book is structured as a working manual: you can read it front to back or jump directly into the part you need for your current deals — from mindset and people at the table to process, negotiations, and drill.

Part What it covers
Part One — The Head What happens the moment an extra zero appears; how big deals are actually perceived; who sells to whom — a price-list merchant or a coordinator of a complex decision.
Part Two — People on Both Sides of the Table Buyer psychology and the seller’s psychology: neither hero nor servant. How roles, fears, and incentives collide in a large transaction.
Part Three — The Process A deal as a route, not a stroke of luck: where people with money really come from, how a conversation is built, how “almost” becomes “yes”, and how pressure and objections show up along the way.
Part Four — Regimen Training and routines without which any technique remains just text. How to keep your own head and skills in shape between big negotiations.
Part Five — The Person Who Runs the Game Frames, positioning, and a sales structure that stops confusing luck with a system. How to see the whole table and run the process instead of reacting to it.
Part Six — Selling to Busy People Lead generation and where money actually remains in your funnel. How to work with people whose calendar is more expensive than your product.
Part Seven — Readiness to Pull the Trigger Preparation, research, dossier, ICP, conversation and meeting structure, diagnostics. Everything that happens before you open your mouth in a key meeting.
Part Eight — Fine-tuning Advanced salesmanship: weaknesses instead of generic “pain points”, constructor of personalized communication, working with objections by disassembling them rather than breaking through.
Parts Nine–Ten — The Final Mile & Practice Negotiating and closing high-ticket deals, delivery and handover, repeat business — plus a practical training section that walks through mindset, structure, pipeline, objections, and regimen so the system stays with you.

About the author

The playbook is written by a practitioner who has spent years in B2B sales and complex deals: from compressors and generators to real estate and investment assets.

The material is based on real negotiations, failed and closed deals, and on what actually happens in rooms where large numbers are discussed — not on abstract “sales theory”.

The goal of the book is simple: make you dangerous in big deals in a good way — so that you understand what is really going on at the table, where the money is lost, and what exactly needs to be done next.

How to work with it

You can treat the book as a reference: read it once to map the territory, then return to specific parts when you prepare for a real deal — mindset, people, process, or final mile.

Chapters in Parts Ten are built as drills and checklists that let you rebuild ongoing deals directly from the text — without templates, scripts marketplaces, or motivational fluff.

Ready to upgrade your next big deal?

Keep the playbook next to your pipeline and use it when a deal appears that is too big to “wing it”.
