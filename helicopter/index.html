How to Sell a Helicopter

A practical playbook on selling anything that hurts to say out loud: helicopters, real estate, yachts, industrial equipment, investment assets, complex services. No pressure tactics, no “guru” clichés — just structure, psychology, and conversations that move big deals forward.

What it is: a system for high-ticket deals — not a “motivation” book.

Where it applies: helicopters and aircraft, real estate, heavy equipment, yachts, investment stones and assets, consulting with six zeroes, complex services.

What you get: mental models, structure of the deal, roles at the table, scripts and questions that actually move the deal forward.

Who this playbook is for

The book is written for people who already know how to sell — and want to turn random big wins into a repeatable system.

Who this is for

  • You sell (or want to sell) things with an extra zero: aircraft, real estate, equipment, luxury goods, investment products, complex services.
  • You already close deals but feel you’ve hit a ceiling on large tickets and can’t scale beyond intuition.
  • You coordinate several stakeholders on the buyer’s side and need a clear map of the decision process, not just “objection handling” tricks.

What you’ll learn

  • What really happens in the buyer’s head when an extra zero appears in the price — and what happens in yours.
  • How to run a deal as a route: from first touch to handover, without losing control at the “almost yes” stage.
  • How to work with risk, power and status in big deals without cheap manipulation, pressure or false friendliness.

What you get inside

  • Clear mental models for big deals: the Head, the People at the table, the Process, and your role as the person who runs the game.
  • Concrete structures: questions, frames, scripts and meeting outlines that you can copy into your CRM and calendar.
  • Practice blocks: drills that let you rebuild current deals and install the system into your daily work instead of “reading and forgetting”.

What’s inside the playbook

The book is structured as a manual: you can read it front to back or jump straight to the parts you need — from mindset and people at the table to process, negotiations and drill.

Part One — The Head What happens when an extra zero appears; how to think about big deals and your position in them.
Part Two — People on Both Sides of the Table The buyer’s psychology and the seller’s psychology: neither hero nor servant; how decisions are really made.
Part Three — The Process A deal as a route, not a stroke of luck: where money comes from, how a conversation is built, where conflict starts, and how “almost” becomes “yes”.
Part Four — Regimen Training and routines without which the book remains just text instead of becoming a working system.
Part Five — The Person Who Runs the Game Frames, positioning, and a sales structure and funnel that stop confusing luck with a system.
Part Six — Selling to Busy People Lead generation for high-ticket deals and where money actually remains in your funnel.
Part Seven — Readiness to Pull the Trigger Preparation, research, dossier, ICP; structure of a conversation and meeting: first touches, diagnostics, and key questions.
Parts Eight–Ten — Fine-tuning, Negotiations, Drill Advanced salesmanship and working with weaknesses (not “pain points”), objections, closing, handover, repeat business, and practical exercises to install the system.
This is not a “daily motivation” book. It is written to give you language, structure and tools for deals where an extra zero changes how everyone behaves — so that buying from you becomes the most rational move in the room.

About the author

The playbook is written by a practitioner who spent years in B2B sales and complex deals: from compressors and generators to real estate and investment assets. The material grew out of real negotiations, failed and closed deals, not abstract “sales theory”.

The goal of the book is simple: make you dangerous in big deals — in a good way. So that you understand what is really happening at the table, where the money is lost, and what exactly to do next instead of improvising.

How to use it in your work

Keep the playbook next to your CRM and calendar. Use it to map the deal, prepare key meetings, rewrite scripts and questions, and de-risk high numbers for the buyer. You don’t have to agree with every idea — but once you start looking at large deals through the lens of roles, risks, and decisions, “selling a helicopter” becomes a logical next step, not a gamble.

Ready to upgrade how you run big deals?

If you work on contracts where one extra zero changes the whole conversation, this playbook gives you a clear way to think, talk and negotiate — from the first touch to handover.
